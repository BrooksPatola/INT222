Video implementation in HTML5

Support for HTML5 video has been growing steady. In June 2013, Netflix added support for HTML5 video. In January 2015, YouTube turned to HTML5 video as it's default player over Flash. In December 2015, Facebook switched from Flash to HTML5 for all video content. The Flash plugin is widely assumed, including by Adobe, to be destined to be phased out, which will leave HTML5 video as the only widely supported method to play video on the World Wide Web.
